Per-OS rates are within-OS only. Don't read across rows: a lower crash count on an OS with lower coverage means we're hearing less, not that it's healthier. macOS/Linux show — because no collector is wired yet (same dashboard, data-only when it lands).
DEX is a read model over the one Guardian event store — it reinterprets crash observations as experience; it does not own a second store. Headline is a measured rate, never a synthetic 0–100 score (that's locked until a 2nd signal — hangs / restarts — exists to composite). Every rate is paired with coverage, and unreachable devices are excluded, not assumed healthy.  — Good direction? Right headline numbers? Anything to add/drop before I port to the dashboard?
